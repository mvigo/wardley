AI Wardley Map Analysis

Wardley Maps are a visualization tool to help understand the current position of various components in a value chain and their evolution over time. In the context of AI, we can use a Wardley Map to represent different AI technologies, techniques, and tools based on their maturity level and the value they provide. Please note that this analysis is a snapshot of the current state of AI as of September 2021, and it may not accurately represent the situation at the time you read it.

Wardley Map of AI Landscape

1. Genesis (Emerging Technologies):

  • Quantum computing for AI
  • Neuromorphic computing
  • General AI (AGI)

2. Custom Built (Early Adoption):

  • AI Explainability
  • AI for edge devices
  • AI in decentralized systems (blockchain)
  • AI-generated art and content

3. Product (Growing Adoption):

  • Natural Language Processing (NLP)
  • Computer Vision (CV)
  • Reinforcement Learning (RL)
  • AI-powered robotics
  • AI-driven cybersecurity
  • AI in healthcare diagnostics
  • AI in finance and trading
  • Voice assistants

4. Commodity (Mature Technologies):

  • Machine Learning frameworks (TensorFlow, PyTorch, etc.)
  • Cloud-based AI services (Google AI, AWS AI, Azure AI, etc.)
  • Supervised Learning algorithms
  • Unsupervised Learning algorithms

Please keep in mind that the AI landscape is constantly evolving, and new technologies and techniques may emerge, while others may advance to different stages in the value chain. Additionally, the map above is not exhaustive, and there may be other AI components not listed here.
